
# Role of NSE
NSE plays an important role in IPO. It's electronic network spans across country providing access to investors in remote areas. NSE provides this infrastructure for conducting online IPOs through **Book Building** process. It operates a fully automated screen based bidding system called **NEAT** IPO. IPO market timing is from **10:00 AM to 5:00 PM**

# Important Documents in IPO
* Prospectus
* Offer Document
* Draft Offer Document
* Red Herring Prospectus
* Abridged Prospectus

## Prospectus
Some of the companies that float public issue may want to exploit the investors. An investor should identify the future potential of these companies before applying for an issue. A part of SEBI guidelines is the disclosure of the information to the public. The disclosure includes information like.
* the reason for raising money
* the way the money is proposed to be spent
* the return expected on the money etc.

Above information is in the form of a **Prospectus**. Prospectus also includes information like.
* the size of the issue
* the current status of the company
* its equity capital
* its current and past performance
* the promoters
* the project
* the cost of the project
* means of financing
* product and capacity
* It also contains a lot of mandatory information regarding underwriting and statutory compliances

These information help investors to evaluate short term and long term prospectus of the company

## Offer Document
**Offer Document** means Prospectus in case of a public issue or offer for sale and _Letter of Offer_ in case of a rights issue, which is filled with the Registrar of Companies (ROC) and Stock Exchanges (SEs). An Offer Document covers all the relevant information to help an investor to make an investment decision.

## Draft Offer Document
**Draft Offer Document** means the Offer Document in draft stage. The Draft Offer Document are filed with SEBI, at least 30 days prior to the registration of a red herring prospectus or prospectus with ROC. SEBI may specify changes, if any, in the Draft Offer Document and the issuer or lead merchant banker shall carry out such changes in the Draft Offer Document before filing the Offer Document with ROC. The Draft Offer Document is available on the SEBI website for public comments for a period of 21 days from the filing of the Draft Offer Document with SEBI.

## Red Herring Prospectus
**Red Herring Prospectus** is a Prospectus, which does not have details of either price or number of shares being offered or the amount of issue. This means that in case price is not disclosed, the number of shares and the upper and the lower price bands are disclosed.

## Abridges Prospectus
**Abridges Prospectus** is a shorter version of Prospectus and contains all the salient features of a Prospectus. It accompanies the application form of public issues.


Merchant Bankers prepare Offer Document and submit to SEBI for approval. Companies cannot sell shares as long as they wish to. There is a provision for Lock-in. **Lock-in** indicates a freeze on the sale of shares for a certain period of time stipulated by SEBI to ensure the control of promoters or main persons contolling the company after the public issue.
