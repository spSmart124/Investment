# Secondary Market

## Introduction
Primary market only deals with fresh issue and their listing. So, issuer and investors are in direct contact. **Secondary market** provides an efficient market for for general investor for trading of securities. For the management of the company, the secondary market work as a channel for monitoring and control by facilitating
* value-enhancing control activities
* implmentation of incentive based management contracts
* aggregation of information via price discovery that guides management decision

Secondary market provides a platform where securities are traded after initially issued to the public in the Primary market. In the secondary market, sellers and buyers meet directly without meeting the issuer as the securities which are bought and sold are those which are already listed. Secondary market comprises of
* Equity Market
* Debt Market

Secondary market is a regulated market where all transactions are made through stock exchange thus making it as safe platform for investors.

## Role of Stock Exchange
Under the guidance of SEBI, stock market works as a platform for buyers and sellers trading in securities. NSE (Nation Stock Exchange) provides an electronic platform where sellers and buyers do not need to meet at a physical location but they can transact through computerised trading screen or internet based trading facility available with the NSE trading member.

Earlier in stock exchanges, the broker member were both the owners and the traders. They also used to manage the exchange it's working. There was a time when it lead to conflict of interest in decision making. Now the exchange are demutualised. Demutualisation refers to legal structure of an exchange where owner, manager and traders are segregated from one another thus providing a bias free environment.
