# Secondary Market

## Introduction
Primary market only deals with fresh issue and their listing. So, issuer and investors are in direct contact. **Secondary market** provides an efficient market for for general investor for trading of securities. For the management of the company, the secondary market work as a channel for monitoring and control by facilitating
* value-enhancing control activities
* implmentation of incentive based management contracts
* aggregation of information via price discovery that guides management decision

Secondary market provides a platform where securities are traded after initially issued to the public in the Primary market. In the secondary market, sellers and buyers meet directly without meeting the issuer as the securities which are bought and sold are those which are already listed. Secondary market comprises of
* Equity Market
* Debt Market

Secondary market is a regulated market where all transactions are made through stock exchange thus making it as safe platform for investors.

## Role of Stock Exchange
Under the guidance of SEBI, stock market works as a platform for buyers and sellers trading in securities. NSE (Nation Stock Exchange) provides an electronic platform where sellers and buyers do not need to meet at a physical location but they can transact through computerised trading screen or internet based trading facility available with the NSE trading member.

Earlier in stock exchanges, the broker member were both the owners and the traders. They also used to manage the exchange it's working. There was a time when it lead to conflict of interest in decision making. Now the exchange are demutualised. Demutualisation refers to legal structure of an exchange where owner, manager and traders are segregated from one another thus providing a bias free environment.

## Stock Trading
Before **Screen Based Trading System (SBTS)** Open-Outcry system was used. Below are the difference between these two

| SBTS   | Open-Outcry System |
| :------- | :------ |
| Effecient | In-efficient |
| Immediate match result | No immediate match result |
| Transparent | Not transparent |


NSE introduced the fully automated system SBTS for nation wide transaction. A buy or sell order is executed automatically as soon as there is a matching counter party buy or sell order of matching quantity and price. NSE is the first exchange in the world to use satellite communication technology. The trading system called **Nation Exchange for Automated Trading (NEAT)** is a client server based application.

For trading in NSE, an investor has to contact a broker through interent, phone, SMS or any other means as defined in the model agreement. Most NSE broker provides internet based trading facilities to their client.

A **contract note** is a confirmation of trades done by a trading member on a particular day. It imposes legally enforceable relationship between the client and the trading member with respect to the trading. It is a prerequisite for filing complaint against a trading member.

As per SEBI, maximum brokerage that can be charged by a broker is 2.5%.

Trading outside a recognized stock exchange is unsecured. Trading in recognized stock exchanges is safe.

## Products in Securities Market
Main Financial products in Securities Market are
* Shares
* Bonds

### Shares
Shares, commonly referred as ordinary shares, represents a fractional owner of a company of a person or entity in a business venture.

Here are the type of shares based on their features.
* Equity Shares: Represents a fractional ownership of a business venture
* Rights Issue/Rights Shares: These shares issued to an existing share holder at ratio of shares and price already held by the investors
* Bonus Shares: These shares are provided to investors free of cost based on the no of share held by the investor
* Preference Shares: It is fixed rate divident or divident calculated at a fixed rate periodically before divident can be paid per equity shares
* Cumulative Preference Shares: It is a type of Preference Share in which divident accumulative if remains unpaid
* Cumulative Convertible Preference Shares: It is a type of Preference Share where the divident payable on the same accumulates if not paid



### Bonds
Bonds are negotiable certificates evidencing indebtedness. It is normally unsecured. It is generally issued by a company, municipalities or government agencies. A bond investor lends money to the issuer and in exchange the issuer promises to repay the loan amount on a specified maturity date. The issuer usually pays the bond holder periodic interest payment over the life of the loan.

Types of Bonds based on their feature
* Zero Coupon Bonds: These are issued at discount and repaid at face value. No periodic interest is paid
* Convertible Bonds: It provides investor option to convert bond to equity at a conversion price
* Treasury Bills: It is short term that is upto 1 year bearer securities issued by government as a means of finance meeting their cash requirement.


Investment in equity provides best result when invested in long term.

## Important Terms
### Growth Stocks
Companies with excellent sales growth and earning potential usually grow faster their peer in the same industry are called **Growth Stocks**. These company usually pay little to no divident instead the invest their profits for their business expansions.

### Bid Price
The **Bid** is the buyer's price. It is this price that you need to know when you have to sell a stock. Bid is the rate/price at which there is a ready buyer for the stock, which you intend to sell.

### Ask Price
The **Ask or Offer** is what you need to know when you are buying. It is means there is a seller who is ready to sell his stock at this price. The seller will sell his stock if he gets the quoted Ask price.


The highest Bid price is called as **Best Bid Price**. The lowest Ask price is called as **Best Ask Price**.

```
Bid-Ask Spread = Best Bid Price - Best Ask Price
```

**Bid-Ask Spread** represents liquidity in the stock. The less the difference the highly traded or liquid is the stock.

### Portfolio
A **Portfolio** is a combination of different investments, mixed and matched, to meet an investor's goal or goals. It can include shares, debentures, bonds, mutual fund units etc. and even real estate asset, arts owned by investor. It generally signifies an investment in financial instruments like shares, debentures, fixed deposits and mutual fund units.

### Value Stocks
These are the stocks that are overlooked by other investors. They have hidden value. These companies might be beaten down in price due to some bad events. Value investors buy undervalued stocks and hold those until the real value of the company's assets are realized.

### Diversification
It is a risk management technique that mixes an wide variety of investments within a portfolio. It is designed to minimize the impact of any one security on overall portfolio performance. It is the best way to reduce risk in portfolio.

#### Advantages of diversification
* A good investment portfolio is a mix of wide range of asset class
* Different securities perform differently at any point in time
* Mix of asset types reduces the impact of a decline in any one security
* If stocks go down, bonds in the portfolio will maintain the balance
* Reduce the risk of your entire portfolio getting affected by decline in any single asset class

## Debt Instruments
It presents a contract between lender and borrower in fixed terms about the periodicity of repayment, interest rate, and principal amount. In indian securities market, the debt instruments issued by State and Central Government are called **bonds**. While debt instruments issued by private sector are calleds **debentures**.

Each debt instrument has 3 features.
* Maturity
* Coupon
* Principal

### Maturity
It refers to the date on which it matures. Term-to-Maturity refers to the number of years remaining for the bond to mature.

### Coupon
It refers to the periodic interest payments that are made by the borrower to the lender. **Coupon rate** is the rate at which interest is paid and is usually represented as a percentage of the par value of a bond.

### Principal
It is the amount borrowed.

```
Coupon = Principal * Coupon Rate
```

## Derivatives
As the terms refers, the value of **Derivatives** is derived from it's underly assets like Bond, Share, Contract, Bullion, Live Stocks etc. It does not have any independent value.

### Definition
With Securities Laws (Second Amendment) Act,1999, Derivatives has been included in the definition
of Securities. The term Derivative has been defined in Securities Contracts (Regulations) Act, as:-
Derivative includes: -
a. a security derived from a debt instrument, share, loan, whether secured or unsecured, risk
instrument or contract for differences or any other form of security;
b. a contract which derives its value from the prices, or index of prices, of underlying securities;

### Types of Derivatives
Below are the types of derivatives
* Forward Contracts
* Future Contracts
* Options
* Warrant

#### Forward Contracts
It is a customized contract between two entities, where settlement takes place on a specific date, in the future, at today's pre-agreed price.

#### Futures Contracts
It is an agreement between two parties to buy or sell an asset at a certain time in the future at a certain price. Futures contracts are specialized forward contracts, in the sense that, they are standardized exchange traded contracts, such as futures of Nifty 50 index.

#### Option
An option is a contract which gives right, but not obligation, to buy or sell the underlying on a stated date and at a stated price. It is executed on or before a future date

Options are of two types
* Calls: It gives the buyer the right to buy an underlying asset but not an obligation
* Puts: It gives the buyer the right to sell an underlying asset but not an obligation


#### Warrant
Options generally have a term of upto 1 year. Maximum maturity of option in stock exchange in of 9 months. Longer dates options are called **Warrants** and are generally traded over the counter.
