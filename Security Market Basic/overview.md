## Security Market
It is a channel for allocation savings to those who have a productive need for them. It has two markets
1. Primary Market
1. Secondary Market


Here is an illustration of the same.
![Securities Market](../resources/primary_secondary_market.png)


### Primary Market
Companies sell new stocks and bonds to the public for the first time. When companies wants to raise funds they issue **securities** to the **investors** who are the savers with the help of **merchant bankers**. Investors provide fund to the company through these bankers. All operations are governed and regualted by **SEBI** (Security and Exchange Board of India).

Here is a pictorial representation of these events.
![Primary Market](../resources/primary_market_architecture.png)

It provides an opportunity to issuer of securities both government and corporations raise resources to meet their requirements of investment.

Securities in the form of equity or debt can be issued in **Internation/Domestic markets** at **face value**, **discount** or **premium**.

Primary market issuance is done through **public issues** or **private placements**.

As per **Company Act 2013**, issue is referred as public if it results in allotment of securities to **50 or more investors**.

When issuer makes an issue of securities to select group of persons not exceeding 49 then it is called a **Private Placement**

### Secondary market
Market where securities are traded after being offered to the public in the primary market or listed on the Stock Exchange.

Once the securities are listed in stock exchange investors can buy securities from each others with the help of **Intermediaries** (Brokers, Dealers). Order for buying and selling are placed with these intermediaries and in turn place order with stock exchange. The exchange then matches the orders that match the order buy/sell quantity and the price. When there is a match the order is executed. After the trade is executed funds/trades are exchanged by the ivestors through the stock exchange and the clearing and settlement houses.

Secondary market comprises of 3 markets
1. Equity
1. Derivatives
1. Debt

The above markets are operated through two mediums
1. Over the Counter (OTC)
1. Exchange-Traded Market

#### Over the Counter (OTC)
It is an informal market where trades are negotiated privately between the parties to the transaction.

#### Exchange-Traded Market
It is a formal systematic market where standardised trades are executed through an exchange.
