## Key Indicators of Securities Markets
1. Index
1. Market Capitalisation
1. Market Capitalisation Ratio
1. Turnover
1. Turnover Ratio

### Index
It is used to give information about the price movements of products in:
* Financial
* Commodities
* Other markets

Indices meant to capture overall behavior of equity markets. It is created by selecting a group of stocks that are representative of the whole market, specified sector or segment of market.

Example: The blue chip index of NSE is the Nifty 50.

### Market Capitalisation
It is defined as value of all listed shares on the country's stock exchanges. It is computed on a daily basis.

Market Capitalisation = Closing price of share * No of outstanding shares

Market capitalisation of all companies listed on a stock exhange is the aggregate of market capitalisation of all companies traded on the exchange.

### Market Capitalisation Ratio
Market Capitalisation Ratio = Market capitalisation of stocks / GDP

It is used as a measure of stock market size.

### Turnover
Turnover = Traded quantity * Trade price per share

Turnover of companies listed on stock exchage is aggregate of the traded value of all companies traded on the exchange.

### Turnover Ratio
Turn Ratio = Total value of shares traded on country's stock exchange for a particular period / Market Capitalisation at the end of the period

It is used as a measure of trading activity or liquidity in the stock markets.

Turnover Ratio of a exchange = Turn over at exchange / Market capitatlisation at exchange

## Products in Securities Markets
Financial markets facilitate reallocation of capital from savers to enterpreneurs. Savings are linked to investments by a variety of intermediaries through a range of complex financial products called **securities**.

Under the Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act [SC(R)A], 1956, **securities** include:
* Shares, bonds, scrips, stocks or other marketable securities of like nature in or of any incorporate company or body corporate.
* Government securities
* Derivatives of securities
* Units of collective investment scheme
* Interest and rights in securities, and security receipt or any other instruments so declared by the central government

Broadly, securities can be of three tryps:
* Equities
* Debt securities
* Derivatives

## Participants
Securities Markets have 3 participants.
* Investors
* Issuers
* Intermediaries

Above participants are regulated by **Security Exchange Board of India (SEBI)**, **Reserve Bank of India (RBI)**, **Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA)** and **Department of Economic Affairs (DEA) of Ministry of Finance**.

### Investors

* Individual Investors
* Corporate Investors

### Issuers
