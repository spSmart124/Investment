# Offer Document
## NFO
NFO stands for **New Fund Offering**. Units in a mutual fund scheme are offered to the public investors through a NFO. There are 7 key steps involved in NFO.

### Step 1
The scheme to be taken to the market is decided by the AMC on the basis on inputs from Chief Investment Officer (CIO) and Chief Marketing Officer (CMO). CIO provides his input on investment objectives. CMO works on the interest of the market on the investment objectives.

### Step 2
The draft Offer Document for the NFO is prepared by the AMC. It is approved by the Trustees and the Board of Directors of the AMC. The trustees must provide an undertaking that the scheme is a new product not a minor modification of existing scheme.

### Step 3
The documents are filed with SEBI. SEBI reviews the draft document and may suggest modifications. Changes suggested by SEBI, needs to be incorporated. If no changes are suggested by SEBI withing 21 days then the draft document is considered final.

### Step 4
The AMC further chooses a suitable time-table for the issue taking into consideration the market situation.


### Step 5
AMC launches advertising and public relations campaigns. These must comply with SEBI's advertising code.

### Step 6
Events for intermediaries and the press are organised by the AMC to raise awareness about the scheme, features and benefits for the investors.

### Step 7
The Application Forms and Offer Documents are distributed to market intermediaries. These are circulated in the market for investors to apply in the NFO.

## NFO Schemes
### Open Ended Scheme
In an Open Ended Scheme investors can invest in the scheme from NFO open date to NFO close date. Investors can offer their unit for repurchase or buy new units from scheme reopening date onwards.

They can repurchase at repurchase price and buy new units at NAV itself. These prices are announced by AMC on the scheme reopening date.

### Close Ended Scheme
Close Ended Schemes also have an NFO open and close date. But they do not have scheme reopening date. The reason being Close Ended Scheme does no sell or repurchase units. Investors buy or sell units in the stock exchanges where the scheme is listed. 


As per SEBI guidlines, NFO except ELSS can be open for upto 15 days only. Unit allotment or money refund must be done withing 5 business days of closure of the scheme. Moreover, Open Ended Scheme must reopen for sale or repurchase within next 5 business days of allotment of units.

## Offer Documents
Offer document provides the details of the scheme in which investors wish to invest. It can be in the NFO of the scheme or it can be in the continuous offer period in case of Open Ended Scheme.

As per SEBI guidlines, an investor needs to be provided all the information in the Offer Document to make an informed decision about the investment. These are:
* The nature of the scheme
* The terms of the offer
* Scheme's investment objectives and strategy liquidity
* The services available to the investor

The Offer Document is the most essential source of information. It details all the core aspects of the scheme such as.
* Type of scheme
* Investment objectives and Investment Pattern of the scheme
* Terms of the issue

### Type of scheme
The type of fun can be.
* Open Ended
* Close Ended
* Interval

Or
* Debt Funds
* Equity Funds
* Hybrid Funds

### Investment objectives and Investment Pattern of the scheme
It covers investment objectives such as
* Growth
* Income
* Both

It also covers investment pattern such as.
* The indicative break-up of the portfolio between equity, debt and money market instruments including min and max percentage of investment.
* The fund has right to alter the allocation for defensive consideration.

In post-investment these documents can be referred to review.
* Investment objectives
* Commitments by AMC
* Adherence to Commitments

The investor must not that their investment is governed by the principle of **caveat emptor** i.e. let the buyer beware. An investor is presumed to have read and understood the Offer Document before investing in it.

### Terms of the issue
Terms of the issue includes
* Liquidity Provisions such as purchase, reissue and redemption.
* Fees and expenses charged to the scheme
* Any safety net or guarantee provided

## Parts of Offer Document
There are two parts in offer document
* Scheme Information Document (SID): SID has details of the particular scheme.
* Statement of Additional Information (SAI): SAI has offering of the scheme, that is, the statutory information about the mutual fund or AMC.

* Though legally SAI is part of the SID, these are two separate documents.
* Both documents are prepared and submitted to SEBI in the format prescribed by SEBI.
* These documents are prepared in simple language, and in clear, concise and easy to understand style.
* The Offer Documents are "vetted" by SEBI, though SEBI does not "approve" them formally.
* Though SEBI does not approve the offer document, it gives its observation.
* This observation is to be incorporated into the Offer document by the mutual fund.

### Update of SID
#### Regular Update
* SID must be updated every year.
* For example, suppose a scheme is launched in the first 6 months of the financial year (say, May 2018), then the first update of the SID is due within 3 months of the end of the financial year (i.e. by June 2019).
* If a scheme is launched in the second 6 months of the financial year (say, December 2018), then the first update of the SID is due within 3 months of the end of the next financial year (i.e. by June 2020).

#### Need-Based
Apart from the regular update, in case there are any changes in the fundamental attributes, the SID should be updated immediately after the lapse of the time period given to existing investors to exit the scheme.
* If there is any other change, then:
* It will be printed on a separate piece of paper (addendum) and distributed along with the SID until the SID is updated.
* If a change is superseded by a further change (for instance, change in load), then addenda are not required for the superseded change i.e. addenda is only required to disclose the latest position.
* The change is to be advertised in an English newspaper having nation-wide circulation, and in a newspaper of the language of the region where the head office of the mutual fund is located.

### Update of SAI
* Regular Update of SAI to be done by the end of 3 months of every financial year.
* Material changes to be updated on an ongoing basis and uploaded on the websites of the mutual fund and AMFI.

### Labelling of Mutual Fund Products
Labelling of Mutual Fund Products can help the investor in easy identification and in meeting their investment objectives.
* Nature of scheme
* A brief about the investment objective
* Riskometer

#### Nature of scheme
* Nature of scheme indicates what kind of product it is
* Whether it is to create wealth or provide regular income in the defined time period (short/medium/long term).
* For example, the statement for an equity fund can be, 'the product is suitable for investors
* For example: The statement for an equity fund can be

The product is suitable for investors who are seeking long-term capital appreciation. 

#### A brief about the investment objective
* Briefing is done in a single line sentence followed by a type of product in which an investor is going to invest, that is, Equity or Debt etc.
* For Example: The description of investment objective for a balanced fund will be:
* A Balanced fund aiming for long-term capital appreciation and current income by investing in equity as well as fixed income securities.

#### Riskometer
* Riskometer depicts a pictorial representation of the risk to the principal invested in a mutual fund product to help investors to take an informed decision regarding their investments.
* This riskometer will represent the risk in the scheme and will fall under one of the five levels of risk as shown in the table.
* The on screen riskometer denotes that the principal will be at moderate risk. It is recommended that investors must consult their financial advisor in case of any doubt about the suitability of the product for their investment needs.

### Disclosure of Products Labels
The product labels should be disclosed in three major places:
* Front page of initial offering application forms, KIM and SIDS.
* Common application form along with the information about the scheme.
* Scheme advertisements

### Key Information Memorandom (KIM)
#### Role of KIM
* A summary of the SID and SAI
* Contains the key information of the offer document
* Should be attached to every application form.

#### Update of KIM
* KIM should be updated at least once a year.
* KIM should be revised if there is any change in fundamental attributes.
