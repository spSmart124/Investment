# Miscellaneous
## SEBI Regulations
### Role of SEBI in Mutual Fund Regulation
Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) controls
1. Mutual Funds
1. Depositories
1. Custodians
1. Registrars and Transfer Agents

The stock exchanges are regulated by SEBI. Every individual stock exchange has rules for
1. Listing
1. Trading
1. Margining

Mutual funds must fulfill the rules of exchanges with a business relationship. Anybody who is unfairly treated can appeal in **Securities Appellate Tribunal (SAT)**.

RBI controls the money market and foreign exchange market in India.
Thus Mutual fund fulfills regulations from RBI regarding
1. Money Market Investments
1. Foreign Currency Payments
1. Investments outside the country
1. Investments from individuals other than Indian residents

#### Investment Limits
SEBI has set limits for investment by schemes of different types of instruments. Refer to below table.
| Investment in | Max Limit (as percentage of NAV) |
| ------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Rated Debt instruments of a single issuer | 10% (may be extended to 12% with prior approval of Board of Trustees and Board of AMC) |
| Unrated paper of a single issuer | 10% |
| Total investment in unrated papers | 25% |
| Money market instruments | 30% (Not applicable to G-Sec, T-bills and CBLOS) |
| Total investment by a fund in a company's paid up capital carrying voting rights | 10% |
| Equity shares or related instruments of a single company | 10% (not applicable in case of index fund or sector specific scheme) |
| Open ended Schemes | 5% of net assets |
| Close ended Scheme | 10% of net assets |
| Debt schemes of mutual funds in a particular sector | 25% of the net assets (excluding investments in Bank CDs, CBLO, G-Secs, T Bills, short term  deposits of scheduled commercial banks and AAA rated securities issued by Public Financial  Institutions and Public Sector Banks) |
| Debt schemes of mutual funds in a group | 20% (excluding investments in securities issued by Public Sector) |
