# Rights and Obligations of Investors
Investor has both rights and obligations in a mutual fund.

## Rights of Investors
### Fiduciary Right
Investors are mutual, beneficial and proportional owners of the scheme's assets. The investment are held by the trust in fiduciary capacity. The fiduciary duty is a legal relationship of confidence or trust between two or more parties.

### Dividend Right
In case of dividend declaration, investors have a right to receive dividend within 30 days of declaration.

### Redemption Right
On redemption request by investors, the AMC must dispatch the redemption proceeds within 10 working days of the request. In case the AMC fails to do so, it has to pay an interest @ 15%. This rate may change from time to time subject to regulations.

### Information Right
Investors can obtain relevant information from the trustees and inspect documents like trust deed, investment management agreement, annual reports, offer documents, etc. They must receive audited annual reports within 6 months from the financial year end.

It is not mandatory for the fund house to distribute the OD with each application form but if investor asks for it, the fund house has to give it to the investor. However, an abriged version of the OD, known as Key Information Memorandum (KIM) has to be provided with the application form

### Closure Right
Investors can wind up a scheme or even terminate the AMC if unit holders representing 75% of scheme's assets pass a resolution to that respect.

### Fundamental Change Information Right
Investors have a right to be informed about changes in the fundamental attributes of a scheme. Fundamental attributes include type of scheme, investment objectives, policies and terms of issue.

### Grievance Right
Investors can approach the investor relations officer for grievance redressal. In the case where they do not get an appropriate solution, they can approach the investor grievance cell of SEBI. The investor can also sue the trustees.

## Obligations of Investors
### Reading OD Obligation
The offer document is a legal document and it is the obligation of investors to read the OD carefully before investing. The OD contains all the material information that the investor would require to make an informed decision.

It contains the risk factors, dividend policy, investment objective, expenses expected to be incurred by the proposed scheme, fund manager's experience, historical performance of other schemes of the fund and a lot of other vital information.

### Redemption Obligation
In case the investor fails to claim the redemption proceeds immediately, then the applicable NAV depends upon when the investor claims the redemption proceeds.
