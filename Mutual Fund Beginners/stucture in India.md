## Structure of Mutual Fund in India
The mutual fund industry is governed by the Securities and Exchange Board of India (mutual fund) regulations, 1996 and such other notifications that may be issued by the regulator from time to time. It follows a 3 tier structure with below parties involved
* Sponsor
* Trust
* Trustee Company
* Asset Management Company

### Sponsor
Sponsor is the initial of mutual fund. It is the first tier of mutual fund. A sponsor should have
* carried out the business of financial services for not less than five years
* positive net worth in all the preceding five years
* profits after depreciation, interest and tax in three out of the five preceding years including the fifth year
* contributed/contributes not less than 40% of the net worth of the asset management company

### Trust
Sponsor approaches SEBI, which is the market regulator and the mutual fund regulator, for approval. Once approved by SEBI, the sponsor creates a **Public Trust**. The **Public Trust** is created as per Indian Trusts Act, 1882. The Trust is registered with SEBI after which it is known as the mutual fund.

### Trustees
Trusts have no legal identity in India. So, they can not enter into contracts. Hence the trustees are the the people authorized on behalf of the Trust to carry out contracts. Contracts are entered in the name of trustees.

Trustees role is not to manage the money. Their job is to ensure that the money is being managed as per the stated objectives. They are seen as internal regulators of the mutual fund.

### Asset Management Company (AMC)
Trustees appoint **Asset Management Company (AMC)**. It performs below actions.
* manages the investors' money
* has to be approved by SEBI
* functions under the supervision of its Board of Directors, and the direction of the Trustees and SEBI
